When a bit duration is occupied by symbol 1, the transmitted signal energy is Eb.When the bit duration is occupied by symbol 0, the transmitted signal energy is zero. On this basis, we may express the average transmitted signal energy as

Eav = Eb/2

To hold this condition, the two binary symbols must be equiprobable. In other words, if we are given a long binary data stream, then symbols 1 and 0 occur in essentially equal numbers in that data stream. For this equality to hold, however, there must be no bias involved in the generation of the bit stream, either in favor of symbol 1 or symbol 0.

Binary data stream:

A binary data stream is a sequence of digital bits (0s and 1s) that represents information in a digital form. This information could be anything from text, audio, images, or any other digital content. In the context of digital communication systems, a binary data stream is typically used as the input signal to be transmitted over a communication channel.

Carrier wave:

A carrier wave is a high-frequency sinusoidal waveform that is used to transmit the binary data stream over a communication channel. The carrier wave has a fixed frequency and amplitude, and its sole purpose is to modulate the binary data stream. The carrier wave is typically generated by an oscillator or a waveform generator, and its frequency is chosen to be higher than the highest frequency of the binary data stream to be transmitted.

Amplitude-shift keying:

Amplitude-shift keying (ASK) is a digital modulation technique that uses the amplitude of the carrier wave to transmit digital data. In ASK, the amplitude of the carrier wave is varied to represent the binary data stream. For example, a binary 0 could be represented by a carrier wave with a low amplitude, while a binary 1 could be represented by a carrier wave with a high amplitude.

To transmit a binary data stream using ASK, the binary data stream is first encoded into a series of digital bits, which are then used to modulate the amplitude of the carrier wave. At the receiver end, the modulated carrier wave is demodulated to recover the original binary data stream. ASK is a simple and easy-to-implement digital modulation technique, but it is not very efficient in terms of bandwidth utilization and is susceptible to noise and interference.
