DSBSC Modulation

DSB-SC

In the process of Amplitude Modulation, the modulated wave consists of the carrier wave and two sidebands. The modulated wave has the information only in the sidebands. Sideband is nothing but a band of frequencies, containing power, which are the lower and higher frequencies of the carrier frequency.

The transmission of a signal, which contains a carrier along with two sidebands can be termed as Double Sideband Full Carrier system or simply DSBFC. It is plotted as shown in the following figure.

Principle of DSB

However, such a transmission is inefficient. Because, two-thirds of the power is being wasted in the carrier, which carries no information.

If this carrier is suppressed and the saved power is distributed to the two sidebands, then such a process is called as Double Sideband Suppressed Carrier system or simply DSBSC. It is plotted as shown in the following figure.

DeModulation

Here, the same carrier signal (which is used for generating DSBSC signal) is used to detect the message signal. Hence, this process of detection is called as coherent or synchronous detection. Following is the block diagram of the coherent detector.

In this process, the message signal can be extracted from DSBSC wave by multiplying it with a carrier, having the same frequency and the phase of the carrier used in DSBSC modulation. The resulting signal is then passed through a Low Pass Filter. Output of this filter is the desired message signal.

Let the DSBSC wave be:

s(t)=Acm(t)cos(2πfct)

The output of the local oscillator is:

c(t)=Accos(2πfct+θ)

Where, θ is the phase difference between the local oscillator signal and the carrier signal, which is used for DSBSC modulation.

From the figure, we can write the output of product modulator as

v(t)=s(t)c(t)

v(t)=Acm(t)cos(2πfct) Accos(2πfct+θ)

v(t)=0.5Ac2[cos(4πfct+θ)+cos(θ)]m(t)

v(t)=0.5Ac2cos(4πfct+θ)m(t)+0.5Ac2cos(θ)m(t)

In the above equation, the first term is the scaled version of the message signal. It can be extracted by passing the above signal through a low pass filter.

Therefore, the output of low pass filter is:

v0(t)=0.5Ac2cos(θ)m(t)

The demodulated signal amplitude will be maximum, when θ=0 degree. That's why the local oscillator signal and the carrier signal should be in phase, i.e., there should not be any phase difference between these two signals.

The demodulated signal amplitude will be zero, when θ=±90 degree This effect is called as quadrature null effect.

Bandwidth of DSBSC Wave

We know the formula for bandwidth (BW) is:

BW = fmax - fmin


Consider the equation of DSBSC modulated wave:

s(t) = AcAm cos(2πfmt) cos(2πfct)


s(t) = 0.5AcAm cos(2π(fm+fc)t)+0.5AcAm cos(2π(fc-fm)t)


The DSBSC modulated wave has only two frequencies. So, the maximum and minimum frequencies are fm+fc and fc-fm respectively.

fmax=fm+fc or fmin=fc-fm


Substitute, fmax and fmin values in the bandwidth formula.

BW=fc+fm - (fc-fm)


BW=2fm


Thus, the bandwidth of DSBSC wave is same as that of AM wave and it is equal to twice the frequency of the modulating signal.

Power Calculations of DSBSC Wave

Consider the following equation of DSBSC modulated wave.

s(t) = 0.5AcAm cos(2π(fm+fc)t)+0.5AcAm cos(2π(fc-fm)t)


Power of DSBSC wave is equal to the sum of powers of upper sideband and lower sideband frequency components.

Pt = Pusb + Plsb


We know the standard formula for power of cos signal is:

P = vrms2/R =2vm2/R


First, let us find the powers of upper sideband and lower sideband one by one.

Pusb = Plsb = Am2Ac2/8R


Pt = Am2Ac2/4R


Therefore, the power required for transmitting DSBSC wave is equal to the power of both the sidebands.
